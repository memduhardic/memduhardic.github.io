I am a Water Resources Engineer specializing in hydrology, hydrodynamics, water quality modeling, and climate impact assessment. My work integrates environmental modeling, Earth observation, and machine learning to support basin-scale water management, drought analysis, and lake system assessments.

My research and professional interests focus on:

Climate change impacts on hydrological systems

Integrated lake and watershed modeling

Drought analysis and reservoir operation strategies

Remote sensing applications in water resources

Data-driven environmental decision-support systems

I work on developing reproducible modeling workflows that combine physical process-based models with modern data-driven approaches. My projects often involve hydrological modeling, hydrodynamic simulations, water quality analysis, and geospatial data processing.

Research & Professional Focus
======
This website presents my academic publications, conference contributions, technical projects, and selected modeling workflows. It also documents my ongoing work in:

Integrated hydrological–hydrodynamic–water quality modeling

Earth observation-based irrigation and land-use analysis

Climate model downscaling and uncertainty assessment

Decision-support systems for basin planning

My long-term goal is to advance environmental modeling approaches that bridge scientific rigor and real-world water management challenges.
